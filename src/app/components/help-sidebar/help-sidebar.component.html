📈 What Does the Signal Mean?

Each ETF is analyzed daily using probabilistic programming, a smart AI technique that looks at past price behavior and market trends to estimate whether an asset is:

  • Cheap (💸 signal < 0) — potentially undervalued
  • Expensive (💰 signal > 0) — possibly overbought
  • Neutral (⚖️ signal ≈ 0) — fairly priced

The Signal is shown on a z-score scale — similar to how weather forecasts use percentages to show probability. Most values are between -1 and +1, but during volatile conditions, the signal can spike higher or lower.

🧮 What Should You Do?

💡 The signal automatically adjusts the Asset/Cash Ratio, telling you how much of your portfolio to invest in that ETF:

  • Signal 0 → 100% asset (neutral market)
  • Signal -1 → ~110% asset (buy more, even borrow)
  • Signal +1 → ~90% asset (reduce exposure, hold more cash)
